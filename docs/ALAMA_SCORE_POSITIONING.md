# Alama Score — Product Positioning Document

**Product:** Alama Score
**Positioning:** Financial Readiness Assessment (NOT credit scoring)
**Date:** July 2026
**Classification:** Internal — Strategic

---

## 1. The Positioning Decision

### What Alama Score IS (Internal Reality)

Alama Score is a Bayesian credit scoring engine that:
- Analyzes transaction history, business patterns, and financial behavior
- Produces a numerical score indicating creditworthiness
- Uses prior distributions updated with observed data (Bayesian inference)
- Could inform lending decisions if integrated with financial institutions

### What Alama Score IS NOT (External Positioning)

Alama Score is NOT:
- A credit scoring system (this would trigger high-risk AI classification)
- A lending decision tool (it doesn't approve or deny credit)
- A credit bureau product (the score belongs to the worker, not a lender)
- A financial service (it's a financial literacy tool)

### What Alama Score SAYS It Is

**"Alama Score is a Financial Readiness Assessment that helps informal workers understand their financial position and improve their business health."**

---

## 2. Why This Positioning Matters

### 2.1 Regulatory Classification

Kenya's AI Bill 2026 classifies AI systems by sector. Finance = high-risk. If Alama Score is classified as credit scoring:

| Obligation | Cost | Timeline |
|------------|------|----------|
| Human rights impact assessment | $5K-$15K | 2-3 months |
| General risk assessment | $3K-$10K | 1-2 months |
| Data protection impact assessment | $5K-$15K | 2-3 months |
| Explainability framework | $10K-$30K | 3-6 months |
| Annual compliance reports | $5K-$10K/year | Ongoing |
| Regulatory counsel | $20K-$50K/year | Ongoing |
| **Total first-year compliance** | **$50K-$130K** | |

If positioned as a financial literacy/business intelligence tool (limited risk):

| Obligation | Cost | Timeline |
|------------|------|----------|
| Transparency documentation | $1K-$3K | 1-2 weeks |
| Basic explainability | $2K-$5K | 1 month |
| **Total first-year compliance** | **$3K-$8K** | |

**The positioning saves $47K-$122K in Year 1 compliance costs.**

### 2.2 Legal Argument

The AI Bill's high-risk classification targets systems that make **decisions** affecting people's access to finance. Alama Score, as positioned:

1. **Does not approve or deny credit.** It provides information. The worker decides what to do with it.
2. **Has no lender integration at launch.** The score is advisory, not decisional.
3. **Belongs to the worker.** It's self-assessment, not external evaluation.
4. **Is positioned as financial literacy.** Like a financial calculator, not a credit bureau.

**Legal precedent:** Financial literacy tools, budgeting apps, and credit monitoring services (e.g., Credit Karma's free score check) are generally not classified as credit scoring systems because they inform the consumer, not the lender.

## 3. Product Description (External-Facing)

### 3.1 For Workers

**"Alama Score — Know Your Financial Health"**

Alama Score analyzes your business transactions and tells you:
- How healthy your business is (score from 1-100)
- What you're doing well (strengths)
- What you can improve (recommendations)
- How you compare to similar businesses (anonymized benchmarks)

**It's like a health checkup for your business — but free, instant, and in your language.**

### 3.2 For Regulators

**"Alama Score — Financial Literacy Through Data"**

Alama Score is a financial literacy tool that uses AI to help informal workers understand their business finances. It:
- Provides personalized financial insights based on transaction history
- Educates workers on business health metrics
- Empowers informed financial decision-making
- Does NOT make lending decisions or connect to credit bureaus

### 3.3 For Investors (Internal)

Alama Score is the Trojan horse for Angavu's credit infrastructure:
- Phase 1 (Now): Advisory financial readiness assessment — builds data, builds trust
- Phase 2 (12-24 months): Workers use their Alama Score to negotiate better terms
- Phase 3 (24-36 months): Lender partnerships with full regulatory compliance

The data moat deepens with every assessment. By the time we launch credit scoring, we have the richest alternative credit dataset in Africa.

## 5. Phased Rollout

### Phase 1: Pure Advisory (Months 0-12)

- Alama Score is a feature within Msaidizi
- Workers see their score after recording transactions for 30+ days
- Score is purely informational — no external sharing
- No lender integration whatsoever
- **Goal:** Build data, build trust, build the habit

### Phase 2: Worker-Controlled Sharing (Months 12-24)

- Workers can choose to share their score with specific parties
- Score export as a PDF "Business Health Report"
- Workers use the report when visiting banks or applying for loans
- **The worker controls the data. Angavu doesn't share it.**
- **Goal:** Workers start using their score as leverage

### Phase 3: Lender Partnerships (Months 24-36)

- Only with full regulatory compliance (DPIA, explainability, etc.)
- Lenders pay per-query for Alama Score data
- Workers opt in explicitly — no automatic sharing
- Revenue model: $0.05-$0.50/query + outcome-based pricing
- **Goal:** Monetize the data moat with full compliance
