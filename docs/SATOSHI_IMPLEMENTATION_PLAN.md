# Satoshi Strategy — Implementation Plan

**Date:** July 2026
**Timeline:** 36 months (3 phases)
**Goal:** Build unstoppable scale through quiet execution, not loud positioning.

---

## Phase 1: The Trojan Horse (Months 0-12)

### Objective
Launch Msaidizi as a simple, helpful voice assistant. Build user base silently. Establish compliance baseline. Nobody outside Angavu knows what's being built.

### Month-by-Month

| Month | Milestone | Key Actions |
|-------|-----------|-------------|
| 1-2 | Foundation | ODPC registration; privacy policy; consent flows; DPO appointment |
| 3-4 | Msaidizi Beta | Launch with 100 beta testers in Nairobi; wire voice input for onboarding |
| 5-6 | Iterate | Fix onboarding based on beta feedback; add Sheng support; optimize for $50 phones |
| 7-8 | Soft Launch | Open to 1,000 users in Nairobi; word-of-mouth only; no press |
| 9-10 | Stabilize | Fix bugs; improve dialect models; add offline sync reliability |
| 11-12 | Scale Prep | Prepare B2B sales materials; build Soko Pulse demo with sample data |

### Success Criteria (Month 12)
- [ ] 5,000-10,000 DAU
- [ ] 10+ dialects working reliably
- [ ] Voice input functional for onboarding
- [ ] ODPC registration complete
- [ ] Zero press coverage (by design)
- [ ] 1-2 B2B pilot conversations started

### Rules for Phase 1
1. **No press releases.** Word of mouth only.
2. **No AI branding.** "Business assistant," not "AI platform."
3. **No credit scoring.** Alama Score doesn't exist publicly yet.
4. **No partnerships.** Focus on the product.
5. **Comply visibly.** Be the first fintech to register with ODPC.

---

## Phase 2: The Quiet Moat (Months 12-24)

### Objective
Launch B2B products. Build regulatory relationships. Deepen the data moat. Still look like a small fintech.

### Month-by-Month

| Month | Milestone | Key Actions |
|-------|-----------|-------------|
| 13-14 | Alama Score Launch | Launch as "Financial Readiness Assessment" within Msaidizi |
| 15-16 | First B2B Client | Close Soko Pulse pilot with FMCG company (Pwani Oil template) |
| 17-18 | Regulatory Engagement | Meet AI Commissioner's office; present Angavu as responsible AI |
| 19-20 | B2B Scale | Close 3-5 more B2B clients; iterate on pricing |
| 21-22 | Strathmore Partnership | Commission independent privacy audit from CIPIT |
| 23-24 | Break-Even | Achieve 7-15 B2B clients; monthly revenue covers costs |

### Success Criteria (Month 24)
- [ ] 100,000 DAU
- [ ] 7-15 B2B clients
- [ ] $400K-$1.2M annual B2B revenue
- [ ] Break-earned achieved (monthly revenue ≥ monthly costs)
- [ ] AI Commissioner relationship established
- [ ] Strathmore CIPIT audit complete
- [ ] "Worker Data Bill of Rights" published

### Rules for Phase 2
1. **Alama Score is "Financial Readiness Assessment."** Never "credit scoring."
2. **No lender integrations.** The score belongs to the worker.
3. **Controlled press.** Only stories about worker impact, not technology.
4. **Regulatory proactive.** Engage before they come to you.
5. **Open-source the privacy layer.** Show transparency without revealing the intelligence layer.

---

## Phase 3: The Reveal (Months 24-36)

### Objective
Scale to millions. Expand to new markets. Launch lender partnerships with full compliance. Become too essential to stop.

### Month-by-Month

| Month | Milestone | Key Actions |
|-------|-----------|-------------|
| 25-26 | Scale | Aggressive user acquisition; target 500K DAU |
| 27-28 | Government Partnership | Angavu Pulse becomes official MSME data source for county governments |
| 29-30 | Nigeria Launch | NDPA-compliant launch; Hausa/Yoruba/Igbo support |
| 31-32 | Lender Partnerships | Alama Score with full compliance; lenders pay per-query |
| 33-34 | Regulatory Sandbox | AI Commissioner sandbox approval for expanded Alama Score |
| 35-36 | Pan-African | Tanzania, Uganda, Ethiopia expansion begins |

### Success Criteria (Month 36)
- [ ] 1M+ DAU
- [ ] 20-40 B2B clients
- [ ] $1.5M-$4M annual revenue
- [ ] Operating in 3+ African countries
- [ ] Government partnership(s) secured
- [ ] Lender partnerships generating revenue
- [ ] Regulatory sandbox approval

### Rules for Phase 3
1. **Scale before revealing.** Millions of users = millions of stakeholders.
2. **Government as customer.** When the government buys your data, they protect your platform.
3. **Regulatory sandbox.** Get approval before scaling Alama Score to lenders.
4. **Expand methodically.** One country at a time. Nigeria first (biggest market).
5. **The reveal is organic.** You don't announce "we're the operating system." The world figures it out.
