## THE INFORMAL WORKER INVISIBILITY THESIS

Kenya's informal economy:
- **83%+ of employment** (KNBS)
- **~35% of GDP** (KNBS) — likely underestimated
- **200M+ workers across Africa**
- **60%+ women**
- **70%+ connected to agriculture**
- **Zero data infrastructure** — invisible in official statistics

**Why they're invisible:**
1. **Institutional failure:** Formal institutions (laws, regulations) don't serve informal workers
2. **Communication failure:** No one speaks their language (literally and figuratively)
3. **Measurement failure:** KNBS surveys miss informal economic activity
4. **Credit failure:** No credit history → no access → no growth

**How Angavu makes them visible:**
1. **Voice-first data capture:** Speak in your language, get economic intelligence
2. **Transaction-based measurement:** Real-time economic activity tracking
3. **Alternative credit scoring:** Alama Score uses behavioral data instead of formal records
4. **Community intelligence:** Jamii Insights aggregates individual data into collective power
