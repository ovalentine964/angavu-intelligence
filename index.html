The Problem

83% of Africa's Workforce Is Invisible

600 million informal workers — mama mbogas, boda boda riders, dukawallahs — have no business records, no data visibility, and get the worst deals from banks and markets.

Market Inefficiency

$120–$500 lost per worker per year from pricing gaps and information asymmetry in local markets.

Coordination Failure

$160–$800 lost per worker per year because they have no collective bargaining power.

Credit Exclusion

$330–$1,300 lost per worker per year because banks can't see their business data.

Total impact: $610–$2,600 per worker per year.
For Kenya alone: $10.7B–$46B annually.
