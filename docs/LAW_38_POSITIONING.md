## THE STRATEGIC LOGIC

### Why Fintech?

1. **Familiar category.** Regulators understand fintech. They have frameworks for it. It's boring and safe.
2. **Positive connotation.** "Financial inclusion" is a policy goal, not a threat.
3. **Low regulatory attention.** Bookkeeping apps don't get scrutinized.
4. **Established precedent.** M-Pesa was "just a payments app" — until it was infrastructure.

### Why Not Infrastructure?

1. **"Infrastructure" implies control.** Regulators get nervous about private infrastructure.
2. **"Operating system" implies monopoly.** Antitrust attention.
3. **"Data moat" implies data concentration.** Privacy regulators investigate.
4. **"Coordination mechanism" implies political power.** Governments surveil.

### The M-Pesa Precedent

M-Pesa launched in 2007 as "mobile money" — a simple way to send cash via SMS. It looked like a fintech product. Nobody called it infrastructure.

By 2024, M-Pesa processed $450B annually, served 60M+ users, and was the de facto financial infrastructure of East Africa. Safaricom couldn't be shut down — too many people depended on it.

**Angavu is following the same playbook:**
- Year 1: "Just a business assistant"
- Year 5: "Kenya's most popular financial app"
- Year 10: "The operating system for Africa's informal economy"

By the time anyone calls it infrastructure, it's too late to stop.
