## 3. Outcome-Based Pricing

### 3.1 What Is Outcome-Based Pricing?

Instead of charging for data access (subscription) or per query, you charge based on the **measurable business outcome** the data enables. The buyer pays a percentage of the value created or a fee tied to a specific result.

### 3.2 How It Works in Fintech/Data

| Model | How It Works | Example |
|-------|-------------|---------|
| **Revenue Share** | Take % of revenue generated using your data | Credit scoring: take 0.5–2% of each loan originated using Alama Score |
| **Performance Fee** | Flat fee tied to achieving a KPI | FMCG: $X per % point increase in market share in tracked regions |
| **Success Fee** | One-time payment upon successful outcome | Insurance: $X per policy sold to previously unbanked customers using your data |
| **Savings Share** | Take % of cost savings achieved | Banks: share of NPL reduction attributable to Alama Score |
| **Licensing + Royalty** | Base fee + royalty on products built on your data | API partners: monthly base + per-transaction royalty |

### 3.3 Outcome-Based Pricing Opportunities for Angavu

#### A. Credit Scoring — Revenue Share on Loan Origination

**Mechanism:** Instead of $0.05–$0.50 per Alama Score query, charge 0.5–1.5% of each loan originated using Alama Score as a factor.

**Math:**
- Average informal sector loan: KES 10,000 ($75)
- If Alama Score is used in 100,000 loans/month across partners
- Total loan value: $7.5M/month
- At 1% take rate: $75K/month = $900K/year
- At current query pricing (100K × $0.10 avg): $10K/month = $120K/year
- **Outcome-based = 7.5x more revenue**

**Risk:** Requires proving Alama Score actually reduces defaults. Need A/B testing data.

**Who pays:** Banks, microfinance, digital lenders (Tala, Branch, M-Shwari)

#### B. FMCG — Revenue Share on Distribution Expansion

**Mechanism:** Charge FMCG companies a success fee for each new viable distribution point identified and activated through Soko Pulse intelligence.

**Math:**
- Soko Pulse identifies 500 new viable distribution points per quarter for a client
- Client activates 200 of them
- Average revenue per new distribution point: $5,000/year
- Total new revenue for client: $1M/year
- Angavu take rate: 5–10% = $50K–$100K per client per year

**Who pays:** Unilever, P&G, Coca-Cola, EABL, Pwani Oil

#### C. Insurance — Per-Policy Revenue Share

**Mechanism:** Co-develop micro-insurance products with insurers. Angavu provides risk scoring and distribution intelligence. Revenue share on premiums.

**Math:**
- Micro-insurance premium: $2–$10/month per worker
- If 100,000 Msaidizi workers opt in across multiple insurers
- Monthly premium pool: $200K–$1M
- Angavu take rate: 5–15% = $10K–$150K/month

**Who pays:** Jubilee, Britam, APA, micro-insurance providers

#### D. Government — Tax Revenue Increase Share

**Mechanism:** Angavu helps KRA (Kenya Revenue Authority) identify unregistered businesses and expand the tax base. Success fee based on incremental tax revenue collected.

**Math:**
- Angavu identifies 50,000 previously unregistered businesses
- Average annual tax contribution: $200
- Incremental tax revenue: $10M
- Angavu success fee: 2–5% = $200K–$500K

**Who pays:** KRA, county revenue authorities

#### E. Market Research — Pay-Per-Insight

**Mechanism:** Instead of subscriptions, charge per specific insight delivered. Clients submit questions; Angavu prices based on complexity and data requirements.

**Examples:**
- "What is the average price of cooking oil in 50 markets this week?" — $500
- "Which Nairobi sub-counties showed >10% transaction growth last month?" — $1,000
- "Compare Coca-Cola vs. Pepsi informal channel pricing in Mombasa" — $2,500

## 5. Competitor Revenue Models

### 5.1 Premise Data

**What they do:** Crowdsource data from contributors in emerging markets using a mobile app. Contributors photograph prices, answer surveys, and report on conditions. Premise sells this intelligence to governments, FMCGs, and development organizations.

**Revenue model:**
- **Government contracts:** $500K–$5M per contract for economic monitoring, food security tracking, crisis response
- **FMCG subscriptions:** $50K–$500K/year for market intelligence in hard-to-reach markets
- **Development organization grants/contracts:** $200K–$2M per project for impact measurement
- **Data licensing:** Sell aggregated datasets to research institutions

**Key insight:** Premise has raised $150M+ and is valued at $1B+. Their core value proposition is "ground truth data from places where traditional data doesn't exist" — exactly Angavu's value proposition, but Angavu's data is passive (transaction data) vs. Premise's active (survey tasks).

**Angavu advantage:** Angavu captures *actual behavior* (transactions), not reported behavior (surveys). This is fundamentally more valuable and harder to fake.

### 5.2 GeoPoll

**What they do:** Mobile-based survey and data collection platform operating across Africa, Asia, and Latin America. Used for market research, audience measurement, and public opinion polling.

**Revenue model:**
- **Custom market research:** $10K–$100K per study for FMCG, media, telecom clients
- **Audience measurement:** Subscription-based ratings for TV/radio stations ($50K–$500K/year)
- **AI Data Streams:** Selling voice recordings and datasets for LLM training ($1M+ deals)
- **International development:** Grant-funded research projects ($200K–$5M)
- **Subscription reports:** Regular market intelligence reports ($5K–$20K/year)

**Key insight:** GeoPoll has pivoted to selling data to AI companies (LLM training data). This is a potential avenue for Angavu — the transaction data could train financial AI models.

**Revenue estimate:** GeoPoll generates $20M–$40M/year across all verticals.

### 5.3 JUMO

**What they do:** AI-driven financial technology platform that provides lending and savings infrastructure to banks and mobile money operators in Africa.

**Revenue model:**
- **Platform fees:** % of each loan originated through their platform (typically 2–5% of loan value)
- **Technology licensing:** Monthly platform fees to bank/MNO partners ($50K–$500K/month)
- **Performance fees:** Additional fees based on portfolio performance (NPL rates below threshold)
- **Data & analytics:** Risk scoring and analytics as part of platform offering

**Key insight:** JUMO has originated $5B+ in loans across 10+ African markets. Their model proves that data-driven credit scoring for informal economies is a massive market. Angavu's Alama Score is a direct competitor/complement.

**Revenue estimate:** JUMO generates $50M–$100M/year in platform and performance fees.

### 5.4 M-KOPA

**What they do:** Asset financing company that uses mobile money and behavioral data to underwrite solar panels, smartphones, and other assets for unbanked customers.

**Revenue model:**
- **Asset financing margins:** 20–40% margin on financed assets
- **Data monetization:** Customer behavioral data used to offer additional financial products
- **Insurance cross-sell:** Micro-insurance products bundled with asset financing
- **Credit scoring licensing:** Repayment data used to build credit profiles, sold to banks

**Key insight:** M-KOPA proves that passively collected behavioral/transaction data can underwrite financial products for the informal sector. Their data asset (payment behavior) is similar to Angavu's (business transaction behavior).

**Revenue estimate:** M-KOPA generates $200M+/year, with data being a key enabler of their lending decisions.

### 5.5 Twiga Foods

**What they do:** B2B marketplace connecting farmers to informal vendors (mama mbogas, kiosks). Handles logistics and provides working capital.

**Revenue model:**
- **Marketplace commissions:** 5–10% on each transaction
- **Financing margins:** Interest on working capital loans to vendors (15–30% APR)
- **Data products:** Market price data sold to FMCG companies and agricultural firms
- **Logistics fees:** Delivery charges for supply chain services

**Key insight:** Twiga has real-time pricing data from thousands of informal vendors — similar to Angavu. They've started monetizing this data as a secondary revenue stream alongside their core marketplace business.

**Revenue estimate:** Twiga generates $100M+/year in GMV, with data products as a growing revenue line.

## 7. Data Monetization Ethics & Compliance

### 7.1 Privacy-Preserving Aggregation Principles

All data products MUST follow these principles:

| Principle | Implementation |
|-----------|---------------|
| **Minimum aggregation** | Never release data with fewer than 50 entities per group |
| **k-Anonymity (k≥50)** | Any data point must be indistinguishable from at least 49 others |
| **Temporal smoothing** | Use rolling averages to prevent identification from timing patterns |
| **Geographic masking** | Aggregate to ward/sub-county level minimum; never expose individual business locations |
| **Differential privacy** | Add calibrated noise to datasets to prevent re-identification |
| **Data minimization** | Only include data fields necessary for the specific product |

### 7.2 Anonymization Requirements

| Data Type | Anonymization Method | Standard |
|-----------|---------------------|----------|
| Worker identity | Full pseudonymization (irreversible hashing) | Always required |
| Business identity | Remove names, use coded IDs | Always required |
| Location | Aggregate to ward level minimum | Always required |
| Transaction amounts | Bucket into ranges (e.g., 0–500, 500–2000, 2000+) | Reports & dashboards |
| Time stamps | Aggregate to daily/weekly | Reports & dashboards |
| Product categories | Fine-grained OK (not identifying) | Permitted |
| Payment methods | Aggregate OK | Permitted |

### 7.3 Regulatory Framework — Kenya Data Protection Act 2019

| Requirement | How Angavu Complies |
|-------------|-------------------|
| **Lawful basis for processing** | Consent obtained at Msaidizi app registration; legitimate interest for aggregated analytics |
| **Purpose limitation** | Data used only for stated purposes; no repurposing without re-consent |
| **Data minimization** | Collect only what's needed for service delivery and aggregated products |
| **Accuracy** | Real-time data collection ensures high accuracy; correction mechanisms in place |
| **Storage limitation** | Define retention policies; delete raw data after aggregation period |
| **Security** | Encryption at rest and in transit; access controls; audit logging |
| **Data Protection Impact Assessment (DPIA)** | Required before launching new data products; document risks and mitigations |
| **Registration with ODPC** | Register as data controller and data processor with the Office of the Data Protection Commissioner |
| **Cross-border transfer** | Ensure adequate protection for data shared with international clients; use standard contractual clauses |
| **Data subject rights** | Provide mechanisms for workers to access, correct, and delete their data |
| **Breach notification** | 72-hour notification to ODPC and affected individuals |

### 7.4 Ethical Data Monetization Framework

**DO:**
- ✅ Sell aggregated, anonymized insights that cannot identify individuals
- ✅ Ensure workers benefit from data monetization (revenue sharing, improved services)
- ✅ Be transparent with users about how their data generates intelligence
- ✅ Give users opt-out mechanisms for data monetization (while keeping core service)
- ✅ Conduct regular privacy audits and DPIAs
- ✅ Share data with development organizations for public good (at reduced rates)
- ✅ Invest in data security infrastructure proportional to data sensitivity

**DON'T:**
- ❌ Sell individual-level data to anyone, ever
- ❌ Allow clients to reverse-engineer individual identities from aggregated data
- ❌ Share data with entities that could use it to exploit or harm workers
- ❌ Monetize data without clear legal basis and user consent
- ❌ Create data products that enable predatory lending targeting vulnerable populations
- ❌ Share granular location data that could enable surveillance

### 7.5 Worker Benefit Sharing Model

To maintain ethical standing and user trust, implement a **Data Dividend** program:

| Mechanism | Description |
|-----------|-------------|
| **Cashback on data revenue** | 5–10% of data product revenue distributed to active Msaidizi users |
| **Free premium features** | Workers contributing data get access to premium Msaidizi features |
| **Financial literacy programs** | Fund financial education using a portion of data revenue |
| **Insurance subsidies** | Use data revenue to subsidize micro-insurance for workers |
| **Transparent reporting** | Publish annual data revenue reports showing how revenue is generated and shared |
